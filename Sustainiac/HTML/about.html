Understanding Sustain: Real vs. Fake

Sustain is the ability of a guitar note to ring out over time, and achieving it has long been a goal for guitarists. Real sustain occurs when the vibrations of your guitar strings are reinforced by sound energy, typically from an amplifier. This creates a feedback loop that allows the strings to resonate powerfully and naturally. However, controlling this raw energy can be challenging, much like taming a wild race car.

In contrast, fake sustain is created by effects like compressors or distortion pedals. These tools amplify or process the guitar signal to simulate the effect of sustain, but the result lacks the organic resonance and vibrancy of real string vibration. While fake sustain can be effective in certain scenarios, it never truly matches the immersive quality of real sustain.

The Power of Real Sustain

Real sustain elevates guitar playing to an art form. By mastering sustain techniques, skilled guitarists can create expressive, dynamic solos that resonate deeply with their audience. Turning up amplifier volume to generate feedback sustain is one traditional approach. The amplified sound causes the guitar strings to vibrate in a feedback loop, producing long-lasting, harmonious notes. This method, however, depends on environmental factors like room acoustics and can be unpredictable.

Sustainiac provides a groundbreaking alternative. By directly vibrating the strings through advanced technology, Sustainiac sustainers deliver reliable, controllable sustain without the need for excessive amplifier volume. This means you can achieve powerful sustain whether you're on stage, in the studio, or even playing at home.

How Sustainiac Works

Unlike traditional effects that modify your guitar signal (such as reverb or distortion), Sustainiac sustainers operate directly on the guitar strings themselves. They use a transducer to create feedback sustain by injecting energy into the strings. This feedback loop ensures the strings continue vibrating indefinitely, producing pure, natural sustain.

With Sustainiac, you can achieve the intensity of amplifier feedback at any volume level. This opens up new creative possibilities for guitarists, allowing them to explore sustained notes, dynamic harmonics, and controlled feedback without the limitations of traditional methods.

Electromagnetic vs. Electroacoustic Sustainiac Systems

Sustainiac offers two innovative systems: electromagnetic sustainers and electroacoustic sustainers. Electromagnetic sustainers use pulsating magnetic energy to directly drive the guitar strings into sustained vibration. This method is highly efficient, compact, and precise, making it ideal for players who demand a seamless integration into their guitar's setup.

Electroacoustic sustainers, on the other hand, convert magnetic energy into acoustic vibrations that resonate through the guitar's neck. These vibrations create a natural feedback effect, mimicking the sound of amplifier-driven sustain but with added reliability and versatility. Electroacoustic systems are easy to set up and provide a distinctive, organic sustain experience.
